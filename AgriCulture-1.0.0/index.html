    Disaster Management Explained

    Disaster management encompasses four key phases: mitigation to reduce risks through planning and infrastructure improvements, preparedness via training, early warning systems, and resource stockpiling, response with immediate rescue operations and aid distribution, and recovery focusing on rebuilding safer communities. Modern systems leverage technology like real-time mapping, satellite monitoring, and mobile alerts to coordinate volunteers, track resources, and provide situational awareness during crises.
